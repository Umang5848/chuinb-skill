## Jargon Categories

### 1. Acronyms & Abbreviations

**Pattern Recognition:**
- 3-4 letter combinations (PE, VC, SaaS, B2B)
- Often represent core concepts or metrics
- New entrants frequently confused by these

### 3. Euphemisms & Softeners

**Pattern Recognition:**
- "Restructuring" = layoffs
- "Exploring strategic options" = might sell the company
- "Challenging environment" = bad times

**Teaching Approach:**
- Teach both the term AND what it really means
- Help users decode polite industry-speak

### 4. Insider Expressions

**Pattern Recognition:**
- Phrases only insiders use naturally
- Often signal belonging to the group
- Can be cultural or regional

**Pattern Recognition:**
- Numerical benchmarks
- Comparison standards
- Performance indicators

**Teaching Approach:**
- What does it measure?
- What's considered good/bad?
- How is it calculated?

## Research Techniques for Jargon

### 1. Industry Publications
- Trade magazines and websites
- Look for glossaries or "101" articles
- Note terms that appear repeatedly

### 2. Job Postings
- Requirements sections reveal expected vocabulary
- Job titles indicate hierarchy terms

### 3. Earnings Calls / Press Releases
- How executives describe their business
- Current buzzwords and framing

### 4. Industry Forums / Communities
- How practitioners talk to each other
- Casual language reveals insider terms

### 5. Books by Industry Insiders
- Often include glossaries
- Explain etymology of terms
