# Industry Templates Reference

This document provides specialized templates and research frameworks for different industry categories.

---

## Industry Categories

Different industries require different research emphases:

| Category | Key Focus Areas | Example Industries |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Technology | Innovation cycles, platforms, network effects | AI, SaaS, Semiconductors |
| Finance | Regulations, risk models, market dynamics | PE, VC, Banking, Insurance |
| Consumer | Brands, trends, demographics | Fashion, F&B, Entertainment |
| Industrial | Supply chains, regulations, capital intensity | Manufacturing, Energy, Logistics |
| Creative | Talent, IP, distribution | Film, Music, Publishing, Art |
| Services | Expertise, relationships, reputation | Consulting, Legal, Healthcare |

---

## Template: Technology Industries

### Research Focus
- [ ] Technology stack and evolution
- [ ] Key patents and IP holders
- [ ] Platform dynamics (if applicable)
- [ ] Regulatory landscape
- [ ] Talent concentration (where do experts work?)

### Key Metrics to Understand
- TAM/SAM/SOM (Total/Serviceable/Obtainable Market)
- R&D spending as % of revenue
- Patent filings trend
- Developer ecosystem size

### Typical Value Chain
```
Research → Development → Platform/Product → Distribution → End User
    ↑                                              ↓
    └──────────── Feedback Loop ──────────────────┘
```

### Insider Vocabulary Patterns
- Technical specifications (nm, FLOPS, latency)
- Generation/version numbering
- Acronyms for standards and protocols
- "Moat" terminology (network effects, switching costs)

### Key Questions for This Category
1. What's the current technology frontier?
2. Who controls the critical chokepoints?
3. What would disrupt this industry?
4. Where is talent concentrated?

## Template: Finance Industries

### Research Focus
- [ ] Regulatory framework (SEC, PBOC, etc.)
- [ ] Key financial instruments
- [ ] Risk management approaches
- [ ] Fee structures and economics
- [ ] Historical crises and lessons

### Key Metrics to Understand
- AUM (Assets Under Management)
- IRR (Internal Rate of Return)
- Carry/Management fees
- DPI/TVPI (for PE/VC)
- Sharpe Ratio, Alpha, Beta

### Typical Value Chain
```
Capital Sources → Fund Managers → Investment Targets → Value Creation → Exit/Returns
       ↑                                                                    ↓
       └──────────────────────── Distribution ─────────────────────────────┘
```

### Insider Vocabulary Patterns
- Return metrics (IRR, MOIC, DPI)
- Deal terminology (LBO, recap, roll-up)
- Fund lifecycle terms (vintage, deployment, harvest)
- Risk terminology (drawdown, volatility, correlation)

### Key Questions for This Category
1. What are the fund economics?
2. How is performance measured?
3. What are the regulatory constraints?
4. Who are the LPs (Limited Partners)?

## Template: Consumer Industries

### Research Focus
- [ ] Consumer segments and personas
- [ ] Brand landscape and positioning
- [ ] Distribution channels
- [ ] Pricing strategies
- [ ] Trend cycles and seasonality

### Key Metrics to Understand
- Same-store sales growth
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Lifetime value (LTV)
- Brand awareness/consideration
- NPS (Net Promoter Score)

### Typical Value Chain
```
Design/Creation → Manufacturing → Distribution → Retail → Consumer
                                      ↓
                              Marketing/Branding
```

### Insider Vocabulary Patterns
- Channel terminology (DTC, wholesale, omnichannel)
- Pricing terms (MSRP, margin, markdown)
- Consumer behavior terms (consideration set, conversion)
- Trend terminology (viral, aspirational, mass-market)

### Key Questions for This Category
1. Who is the core customer?
2. What drives purchase decisions?
3. How are brands built here?
4. What's the distribution landscape?

## Template: Creative Industries

### Research Focus
- [ ] Content creation ecosystem
- [ ] IP ownership and licensing
- [ ] Distribution platforms
- [ ] Revenue models
- [ ] Talent dynamics

### Key Metrics to Understand
- Box office / streaming numbers
- Rights valuations
- Audience metrics (ratings, streams, engagement)
- Production costs vs. returns

### Typical Value Chain
```
Talent/Creation → Production → Distribution → Consumption → Monetization
       ↑                                                          ↓
       └────────────────── Rights/Licensing ─────────────────────┘
```

### Insider Vocabulary Patterns
- Deal terms (points, back-end, gross/net)
- Production terminology (pre/post, green light)
- Distribution terms (theatrical window, streaming rights)
- Industry-specific slang

### Key Questions for This Category
1. How is content financed?
2. Who controls distribution?
3. How do creators get compensated?
4. What makes a hit?

            ↑                                                                    ↓

2. How are services priced?
3. What drives client selection?

## Universal Research Checklist

Regardless of industry type, always research:

### Market Structure
- [ ] Market size and growth rate
- [ ] Key players and market share
- [ ] Industry concentration
- [ ] Entry barriers

### Competitive Dynamics
- [ ] Porter's Five Forces analysis
- [ ] Key success factors
- [ ] Competitive advantages
- [ ] Disruption threats

### People & Culture
- [ ] Key figures (founders, executives, thought leaders)
- [ ] Career paths and trajectories
- [ ] Industry associations and events
- [ ] Cultural norms and expectations

### Current Events
- [ ] Recent news and developments
- [ ] Emerging trends
- [ ] Regulatory changes
- [ ] Technology shifts
